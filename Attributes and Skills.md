The most common role you'll likely make in your adventure is a skill-check. You roll a d20 and add your skill-modifier. All skills have one or more attributes associated with them. To calculate your skill-modifier take all skill-points you spend on that skill and add one of the attribute-modifiers of an associated attribute to it.

##### Example
> Travis needs to make an intimidation-check. He has a skill-value of 3 on intimidation. His character has +3 strength and +0 charisma. Travis decides to intimidate his opponent with his strength giving him a modifier of 3+3 = 6. He rolls the d20 and gets a 15, adding his modiefier he ends up with a total of 21.

##### Note
> The DM might decide that in a special situation only one of the associated attributes, or even an attribute that is usually not associated with a skill is the ONLY attribute that makes sense to use. In those cases use the attribute the DM chose and add your skill-value
