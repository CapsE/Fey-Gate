The most common role you'll likely make in your adventure is a skill-check. You roll a d20 and add your skill-modifier. All skills have one or more attributes associated with them. To calculate your skill-modifier take all skill-points you spend on that skill and add one of the attribute-modifiers of an associated attribute to it. You'll usually pick your highest attribute so it's recommended to only do this calculation once every time your stats change (like when leveling up).
It's recommended to keep in mind how your character does these checks. If your performance +6 modifier comes from dexterity you're likely to entertain people with juggeling or cartwheels while the DM may decide to make you use your charisma instead if you try to sing an opera.

##### Example
> Travis needs to make an intimidation-check. He has a skill-value of 3 on intimidation. His character has +3 strength and +0 charisma. Travis decided to intimidate his opponent with his strength giving him a modifier of 3+3 = 6. He rolls the d20 and gets a 15, adding his modiefier he ends up with a total of 21.
