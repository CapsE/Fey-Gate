When creating your character pick the different species present in your lineage. It can be just one species or you can go back multiple generation. You decide how much you take after your ancestors and how you look.
Pick three aspects you inherited from your ancestors. You can choose them freely but I gave a few examples of which aspects are usually assoiciated with which species.

## Species
### Human
Humans are the most common species in fantasy settings and are known for their adaptability and versatility. They have no inherent magical abilities, but they are quick learners and excel in a wide range of skills.

### Elf
Elves are a long-lived species with a strong connection to nature and magic. They are agile and dexterous, with keen senses and a natural affinity for archery and sword fighting.
* Nature affinity: You gain +2 in Nature or Survival
* Magic affinity: You gain +2 in Arcana
* Naturally Dexterous: You gain +2 in sword fighting or archery

### Dwarf
Dwarves are a hardy, mountain-dwelling species known for their strength, endurance, and craftsmanship. They are skilled in metalworking and engineering, and are resistant to poison and magical effects.
* Endurance: You gain +1 to your Constitution modifier
* Craftsman: You are proficient with two types of craftig tools or an expert with one.
* Resistance to Poison: You take half damage from poison 

### Orc
Orcs are a brutal, warlike species known for their strength and ferocity in battle. They have thick skin and strong muscles, and are resistant to physical damage.
* Resistance to Fire: You take half damage from fire
* Ferocity: Your third attack each turn is a critical hit
* Nature affinity: You gain proficiency in Nature or Survival

### Halfling
Halflings are a small, agile species known for their quick reflexes and stealthy nature. Despite their size, halflings are tough and resourceful, able to withstand hardship and adversity. They are also known for their kindness and hospitality, and are often found in communities where they can put their skills to use in helpful ways.
* Nimble: You can pass through enemy's spaces without issue
* Friendly: You gain proficiency in Persuasion
* Resourceful: You may add +2 to your skill check when using the Restore action.

### Kobolds
Kobolds are a small, reptilian race known for their cunning and resourcefulness. They are agile and stealthy, with a natural affinity for traps and ambushes. Despite their small size, kobolds are tough and resilient, able to withstand harsh conditions and environments. They are also skilled at working with metal and stone, and are often found in underground mines and tunnels.
* Cunning: When using the Decieve action you crit with a 18+
* Craftsman: You are proficient with two types of craftig tools or an expert with one.
* Nimble: You can pass through enemy's spaces without issue

### Therian
The Therian species is a race of anthropomorphic animals with both animal and human characteristics. They are known for their strong, agile bodies and heightened senses, as well as their  adaptability. They are often depicted as fiercely independent, with a deep connection to nature and the natural world.

Therians are versatile and skilled in a wide range of activities, including hunting, tracking, and survival. They are also known for their strong sense of community and loyalty to their clans. However, they can also be fiercely territorial and will defend their territory and loved ones with ferocity.

In terms of appearance, Therians can take on a wide range of forms, with some resembling traditional animals such as wolves, bears, and cats, while others may have more exotic or fantastical appearances. Many Therians also have the ability to transform between their animal and human forms, although this ability is not universal to all members of the species.
* Ferocity: Your third attack each turn is a critical hit
* Nature affinity: You gain proficiency in Nature or Survival
* Heightened senses: Choose either sight or smell and hearing. You gain advantage on perception checks made with the choosen senses.

### Starting Attributes
Take the modifiers [+3, +2, +1, +1, +0, -1] and assign each to one of the six attributes. These attributes decide what comes naturally to your character and where they might struggle.  After that you can spend 6 skill-points on any skill though you can't spend more than 3 on a single skill.
